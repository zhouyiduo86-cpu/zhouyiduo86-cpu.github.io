Case Study: Urban Forestry LCA & Greenhouse Gas Economic Optimization

Executive Summary & Deliverables

Conducted as part of the NSERC Alliance Project, this research evaluates the environmental and economic impacts of urban wood waste management in the City of Toronto. By developing a probabilistic Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) model, the study compares the “Business as Usual” (mulching) approach against biochar conversion. The analysis utilizes extensive Monte Carlo simulations to quantify supply chain emissions and forecasts long-term financial impacts using Social Cost of Carbon (SCC) discounting.

Stochastic Modeling & Economic Parameters

To account for supply chain uncertainties, the LCA engine heavily relies on probabilistic distributions (Lognormal and Triangular) across 10,000 simulation draws.

Parameter Distribution Description
Distance (Long/Short) Lognormal Distribution Variable transit routing to processing facilities.
Fuel Economy Lognormal Distribution Heavy machinery and transit fuel consumption variances.
PV Interaction Factor Triangular (Min: 1.0, Max: 1.5) Pavement-vehicle interaction impact.
Carbon Price Scenarios Deterministic Evaluated at C$30, C$65, and C$100 per tCO2.

Monte Carlo Simulation Engine (Python)

Core probabilistic simulation logic from lca_emission_simulation.py. 

import numpy as np
import pandas as pd

n_sims = 10_000
rng = np.random.default_rng(42)

def sample_lognormal_from_range(q_low, q_high, size, rng):
    """Approximate a lognormal distribution utilizing 95% quantiles."""
    log_low = np.log(q_low)
    log_high = np.log(q_high)
    sigma = (log_high - log_low) / (2 * 1.96)
    mu = 0.5 * (log_low + log_high)
    return rng.lognormal(mean=mu, sigma=sigma, size=size)

# Simulate stochastic parameters
FE_long_sim = sample_lognormal_from_range(29.0, 47.0, n_sims, rng)
FE_short_sim = sample_lognormal_from_range(22.0, 32.0, n_sims, rng)
p_sim = rng.triangular(1.0, 1.3, 1.5, size=n_sims)
EF_CO2 = 2.68
